Tourist Places

Prigi Beach is a beach on the southern coast located along the edge of the Indian Ocean, administratively part of Tasimadu Village, Watulimo District, Trenggalek Regency, East Java, Indonesia. It is about 48 km from the center of Trenggalek.

In addition to being a popular tourist attraction, Prigi Beach is also a national port, where it serves as one of the largest fishing ports on the southern coast of Java Island.

Visitors to this beach can enjoy activities such as fishing, camping, playing tennis, or simply staying at one of the nearby hotels to enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Prigi Beach becomes livelier during the month of Selo in the Javanese calendar, as local fishermen hold the traditional Larung Sembonyo ceremony — an annual ritual to give thanks to God for abundant sea harvests. Besides being a thanksgiving ceremony, locals also believe that Larung Sembonyo is held to commemorate a historical wedding of Raden Tumenggung Yudha Negara, a military leader of the Mataram Kingdom who opened the Prigi region with the promise to marry Princess Gambar Inten.

Pelang Beach is located in Wonocoyo Village, Panggul District, about 56 km southwest of Trenggalek City.

Pelang Beach is famous for its large waves, beautiful scenery, and natural charm. About 300 meters northwest of the beach, there is a waterfall with a height of 25 meters. This waterfall can be used for bathing and is believed by locals to help maintain youthfulness. In addition, about 100 meters east of Pelang Beach, there is Pelang Cave with a depth of approximately 500 meters. Pelang Cave is still completely natural and has potential for further development.

To enjoy the beautiful panorama of the sea and Pelang Beach, visitors can climb to the hilltop near the beach, which is equipped with a viewing gazebo, offering a clear and stunning view of the entire area.

Gua Lowo (in Indonesian: "bat cave") is a natural tourist attraction located in Trenggalek Regency, East Java. Gua Lowo is the longest cave in Southeast Asia, located in Watulimo District, Trenggalek Regency, with a total length of approximately 2 km. However, only 850 meters of it can be enjoyed by visitors because the remaining 1,150 meters currently have no access and are blocked by a strong river flow. Gua Lowo is about 27 km from the center of Trenggalek and Tulungagung.

History
This cave was discovered in 1931 by a local resident named Lomedjo. When it was first found, the cave was covered by bushes and surrounded by dense forest. The name "Gua Lowo" or "bat cave" comes from the fact that the cave has long been inhabited by bats, which are called "lowo" in Javanese. Even today, a distinct bat odor can still be detected in several parts of the cave. Although discovered in 1931, the Trenggalek government only planned to develop Gua Lowo as a tourist site in 1983. During the planning stage, the government invited geology experts from Bogor and France, named Gilbert Manthovani and Dr. Robert K. Kho. Their exploration revealed that Gua Lowo was 2 km long and contained very large chambers. After this research, in 1984 Gua Lowo was officially opened as a public tourist attraction. According to Suparlan, Head of Tourism and Culture Promotion at the Trenggalek Youth and Sports Office, research by the French cave experts Gilbert Mantovani and Kingston Kho in 1985 declared Gua Lowo the largest and longest cave in Southeast Asia.

Tapan Bathing Place is a natural pool located in Karangan Village, Karangan District, Trenggalek Regency. Surrounded by trees, it was once popular for its natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere.

The pool is fed by a natural spring, offering visitors a cool swimming experience amid forest scenery.However, some reviews note that access roads and facilities have declined over time, and there are concerns that the site may no longer be open to the public.

History
Originally, the Tapan bathing place, according to my ancestor Mbah Kemis, was a small spring (sendang) surrounded by stones arranged in a circular shape. In the past, the spring was used by people for ritual meditation, bathing, and other needs. Later, in the 1950s, the head of Karangan Village, Ngaidi, expanded it to about five meters while keeping its original physical shape. In 1994, Tapan was officially inaugurated as a tourist bathing site by the Governor of East Java, Basoefi Sudirman. Over time, the shape of the Tapan spring changed from circular to rectangular as it is seen today. Geographically, part of the Tapan area is located in Karangan Village, while another part is in Kerjo Village, Karangan District, Trenggalek Regency.
